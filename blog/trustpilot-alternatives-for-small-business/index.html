The 10 Best Trustpilot Alternatives in 2026 — Ranked by Verification Method

The 10 best Trustpilot alternatives ranked by how they verify reviews — from none to processor-attested. Covers SaaS, e-commerce, local business, and enterprise

2026-07-24

Trustpilot is the largest review platform on the internet — 361 million reviews, roughly 55% market share, and a brand consumers recognize. But it's not always the best fit, and for many businesses it's actively the wrong one. Anyone can post on Trustpilot without buying anything. The "Verified" badge most people associate with Trustpilot means the business invited the reviewer by email — not that a payment was independently confirmed. And pricing starts at $299/month for the tier most businesses actually need.

This guide ranks 10 alternatives on the dimension most comparison posts skip: how each platform verifies a review actually comes from a paying customer. We score every option on a five-level verification spectrum — because "verified" means something wildly different depending on who's attesting to the purchase.

# How we score: the verification spectrum

Every review platform sits somewhere on this spectrum. The column on the right is where verification is structurally impossible to fake — and almost no one operates there.

Level Name Who attests Can it be gamed by the merchant?
0 None No one Trivially
1 Email ownership Email provider Yes — anyone can create an email address
2 Self-attested The reviewer Yes — "I bought this" checkbox
3 Merchant-supplied The merchant's own data Yes — the merchant controls the data source
4 Processor-attested Independent payment processor No — the processor is a neutral third party

# The 10 best Trustpilot alternatives

# 1. Signed Reviews — ★ Best for Stripe businesses

| Verification level | Level 4 — Processor-attested | | Starting price | Free (up to 25 reviews/mo) / $29–$199/mo | | Stripe-native | ✅ Yes — one-click OAuth, read-only | | Review ownership | ✅ You own your reviews | | Best for | SaaS, e-commerce, services on Stripe |

Signed Reviews is the only platform in this list that operates at Level 4 — processor-attested verification. Every review is matched against a real Stripe charge, confirmed independently by Stripe (not by merchant-supplied data), and cryptographically signed so its authenticity can be checked later. Refund a charge? The review hides automatically. No other platform in this list can make that claim — because every other platform verifies against data the merchant supplies, not data an independent processor confirms.

Why it ranks #1: If you process payments through Stripe, this is the only review tool purpose-built for you. The verification model is FTC-compliant by construction — you can't fake a review without faking a real Stripe charge, which costs Stripe fees and risks account closure. The free plan is genuinely usable for small businesses.

Pricing: Free (self-service, 25 reviews/mo) → Starter $29/mo (automation, 100 reviews/mo) → Pro $79/mo (250 reviews/mo) → Scale $199/mo (1,000 reviews/mo).

# 2. Judge.me — ★ Best for Shopify stores

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (Shopify order data) | | Starting price | Free (unlimited reviews) / $15/mo | | Stripe-native | ❌ No — tied to Shopify platform | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Shopify-only stores |

Judge.me is the most-installed review app on Shopify (127 million reviews collected). It verifies against Shopify order data — which means it checks that a matching order exists in the merchant's store. That's strong for Shopify-only businesses, but the attesting party is still the merchant's platform data, not the payment processor.

Why it's #2: It's the best Shopify-native option by volume and simplicity. The free plan includes unlimited reviews. But if you also sell outside Shopify or want processor-level verification, pair it with Signed Reviews.

→ Signed Reviews vs Judge.me

# 3. Yotpo — ★ Best for enterprise DTC

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (MAP email + order data) | | Starting price | $15/mo+ (scales with volume) | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Enterprise DTC brands with budget |

Yotpo is the enterprise default for direct-to-consumer brands. Its "Verified Buyer" badge means the reviewer clicked through a Mail-After-Purchase email and Yotpo matched the email to an order record. The verification is solid — but it's the merchant's MAP flow and order data doing the attesting, not an independent processor. At enterprise scale, Yotpo gets expensive quickly.

Why it's #3: Best-in-class for enterprise DTC who need loyalty, SMS, and subscription integrations alongside reviews. Overkill and overpriced for small-to-mid businesses.

→ Signed Reviews vs Yotpo

# 4. Feefo — ★ Closest positioning competitor

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (transaction feed) | | Starting price | Custom (typically $$-$$$) | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Mid-to-large UK/European businesses |

Feefo is the closest positioning competitor to Signed Reviews on paper — they also lead with "verified reviews" language. But Feefo's verification is done against a transaction log the merchant uploads. The merchant decides what goes into the feed. It's closed (no unsolicited reviews) and Google Review Partner-certified, which is genuinely valuable. But the trust root is still the merchant's data.

Why it's #4: Strong for UK/European businesses wanting Google Seller Ratings. The verification model is better than open platforms, but it's Level 3 — the merchant supplies the data being verified against.

→ Signed Reviews vs Feefo

# 5. Reviews.io — ★ Best for UK businesses wanting strong branding

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (invitation + manual receipt) | | Starting price | $89/mo | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | UK/European businesses |

Reviews.io is the most-recommended Trustpilot alternative in UK e-commerce circles. "Verified Reviewer" status comes through its invitation system using customer data the business provides (a list upload or an integration). It also allows manual receipt verification — customers can submit proof of purchase. Google Review Partner. Good feature set, strong brand.

Why it's #5: Solid all-rounder for UK businesses. The manual receipt verification path is interesting but doesn't scale. Still Level 3 — the business supplies the customer data.

→ Signed Reviews vs Reviews.io

# 6. eKomi — ★ Google Review Partner with transaction language

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (transaction feed) | | Starting price | Custom | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Businesses wanting Google Seller Ratings |

eKomi uses "transaction-verified" language and is a Google Review Partner — meaning your reviews can surface as seller ratings in Google Shopping results. But like Feefo, the verification is against a feed the merchant supplies. Close to the right vocabulary; not quite at the right verification level.

Why it's #6: Google Seller Ratings are genuinely valuable for e-commerce. But "transaction-verified" means the merchant's transaction data — not an independent processor.

→ Signed Reviews vs eKomi

# 7. Stamped.io — ★ Solid multi-platform e-commerce option

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (order data) | | Starting price | $23/mo | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Multi-platform e-commerce |

Stamped.io (formerly Stamped) supports reviews, ratings, Q&A, and visual marketing across multiple platforms. Purchase verification through order matching. A solid mid-market option, and more affordable than Yotpo for comparable features.

Why it's #7: Good value for multi-platform stores. Nothing wrong with it — it just sits at the same Level 3 as everyone else, without a distinctive verification story.

→ Signed Reviews vs Stamped

# 8. Okendo — ★ Best for Shopify brands focused on visual UGC

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (Shopify order data) | | Starting price | $19/mo | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Visual-first Shopify brands |

Okendo is Shopify-native and strong on photo/video reviews and customer attributes (skin type, size, etc.) — useful for beauty, fashion, and health brands. Verification is through Shopify order data. Nice product; verification model is standard Level 3.

Why it's #8: Excellent for Shopify brands that want rich visual reviews with customer attributes. Not a fit outside Shopify.

→ Signed Reviews vs Okendo

# 9. Loox — ★ Best for Shopify product reviews with photo emphasis

| Verification level | Level 3 — Merchant-supplied (Shopify order data) | | Starting price | $9.99/mo | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ✅ Yes | | Best for | Shopify stores wanting photo reviews on a budget |

Loox is a popular Shopify review app known for automated photo review collection and attractive review displays. Good value at $9.99/month. Verification is Shopify order-based — standard Level 3.

Why it's #9: Affordable and effective for Shopify photo reviews. The cheapest option in this list for Shopify-specific needs.

→ Signed Reviews vs Loox

# 10. Google Reviews — ★ Essential supplement, not a replacement

| Verification level | Level 0 — None | | Starting price | Free | | Stripe-native | ❌ No | | Review ownership | ❌ Google | | Best for | Local SEO, all businesses as a supplement |

Google Reviews aren't a Trustpilot alternative in the traditional sense — they serve a different purpose (local SEO, Google Maps visibility, search result stars). But for local businesses, they're the single most important review surface. Anyone with a Google account can leave a review; there's no purchase verification. Best used alongside a verified review platform.

Why it's #10: Essential for local SEO — no business should ignore Google Reviews. But they're Level 0 on the verification spectrum. Supplement them with verified reviews on your own site.

# What about Trustpilot?

Trustpilot's not going anywhere. For some businesses — especially those selling to consumers who search "company name + reviews" — being present on Trustpilot is table stakes. The issue isn't that Trustpilot is useless; it's that Trustpilot alone leaves your review credibility on a platform where unverified reviews sit next to invited ones and the distinction is easily missed.

The smartest approach for most Stripe businesses: use Signed Reviews as your verified-review engine (Level 4, every review backed by a confirmed Stripe charge) and maintain a Trustpilot presence selectively. Your verified reviews live on your own site, your public profile page, and Google via structured data. Trustpilot becomes a distribution channel, not your review strategy's foundation.

# Bottom line

Most "Trustpilot alternatives" lists are really just "review platforms ranked by monthly price." The more important question is: when a review says "Verified," verified by whom?

If the answer is "the merchant," you're at Level 3 — and so is every major platform except one. If you process payments through Stripe, you can operate at Level 4 — processor-attested verification — and offer your customers something structurally more credible than anything a merchant-supplied platform can provide.
